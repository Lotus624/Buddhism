The Method

Grammar in service of reading

Every week pairs one grammatical layer — a declension, a tense, a class of particles — with a genuine passage from the Suttas or commentaries, so the grammar is never abstract: it is always the key to a real sentence spoken 2,500 years ago.

① Nāma — Nouns

Nine cases, three genders, two numbers. Weeks 2–12 walk through every stem class — -a, -i, -ī, -u, -ū, -o, -ā — masculine, feminine and neuter, plus all the pronouns.

② Ākhyāta — Verbs

Present (Vattamānā), past (Ajjatanī), future (Bhavissanti), imperative (Pañcamī) and optative (Sattamī), with roots, absolutives and infinitives.

③ Upasagga — Prefixes

The twenty verbal prefixes and how √hara becomes brings, uses, destroys, lives — the engine of Pāli vocabulary, mastered in Week 10.

④ Nipāta — Indeclinables

Particles, adverbs and euphonic combination (sandhi) — the glue of the language and the final key to parsing canonical text, Weeks 8, 15 & 16.

Week 1 Foundation

The Pāli alphabet — 41 syllables

Pāli has 8 vowels and 33 consonants. Consonants are arranged in five groups (vagga) by where in the mouth they are produced. Select a group to study its letters.
